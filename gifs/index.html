How To Make

Hand-Drawn GIFs

Some Background

The GIF, or Graphics Interchange Format, has become a cultural powerhouse on the internet over the past decade. The looping, mesmeric quality has become a mainstay in how people communicate today, and who wouldn't want to add their own personal touch?

This guide will give you a basic walkthrough on making your own gifs by hand! The basic format is simple—drawing individual frames, then using photoshop to stitch them together, and finally saving them as a GIF. Included are some helpful resources (printable template, photoshop action) to make the process faster & easier:)

You Will Need:

  • Paper
  • A Printer
  • A Scanner
  • Drawing Materials
  • Photoshop
  • Patience

Step One: Draw

  1. Set up your drawing template! We will be using this 32-frame template printed on 2 sides of letter size paper.
  2. Start drawing your GIF frame by frame! If you want your gif to loop, make sure your first and last frame are similar.
  3. The template provided here should be filled out left to right, top to bottom, on both sides, for a total of 32 frames–

Step Two: Animate

  1. Once you have all your frames drawn out, it's time to scan the paper to get a digital copy!
  2. Once you have a digital copy of your frames, Photoshop is where the animation magic happens. We can use this Photoshop action with the template we used earlier to animate all the frames together automatically. To load this action into Photoshop, go to the Action panel, click the menu on the top right, and choose "Load Actions".
  3. Once you have the papers scanned and action loaded, open the scans of the front and back of the paper, crop them to the edge of the paper, and rotate them to landscape mode so that the drawings are right-side-up.
  4. Open both of these scans into one document. There should be two layers in the document: the front and back side of the paper. Make sure the front side is on the top layer, and name the front layer "Front" and the back layer "Back", like so: Also, make sure the image is at 300 Pixels/Inch- you can change this in Image > Image Size.
  5. Open the Action panel and select the action you just loaded called "Make Gif". Press the play icon below it, and wait for a few seconds—your gif should come out!
  6. Save your gif and share it with the world!
