This is a website for making ciphered fonts for visual encryption.

Digital information is always potentially unsecure, or at least it feels that way now; constantly breaking news about government backdoors, hacks of company data. There's an attitude of resignation that any digital content is potentially accessible.

This tool asks, "What if text never existed digitally at all? What if it only existed visually?”

p

The tradition of typewriters, printing, and writing all lead us to expect that when we press the A letter on the keyboard, the letter A will appear on the screen—but it doesn’t have to. This tool lets digital letters correspond with any visual letter. In this way, the font file itself acts as a cipher key.

This means that text would be digitally gibberish to anyone without access to the typeface, but once you have it, the digital gibberish becomes visual, readable language on your screen. You make use of digital communications without text actually existing digitally.

You could make a cipher font to send to your friends, and put text on a website that only looks like language to those who have the font installed on their system.
